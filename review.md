# Review

## Findings

1. High: The new velocity decay and stall heuristic will misclassify normal low-speed motion as a stall, then ratchet commands upward.

   In the wheel firmware, encoder packets are only emitted when the count changes, and the encoder resolution is 60 counts per revolution ([pico_ware_wheels/code.py](pico_ware_wheels/code.py#L12), [pico_ware_wheels/code.py](pico_ware_wheels/code.py#L146), [pico_ware_wheels/code.py](pico_ware_wheels/code.py#L152)). The ros2_control loop runs at 30 Hz ([ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/config/diffbot_controllers.yaml](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/config/diffbot_controllers.yaml#L3)). With the new read path, every cycle without a fresh encoder packet multiplies the estimated velocity by 0.6 ([ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp#L281)), and the write path treats a wheel as stalled once velocity drops below 0.05 rad/s for 15 cycles at commanded speeds under 1.0 rad/s ([ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp#L377), [ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp#L380), [ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp#L386)). At the new minimum effective speed of 0.35 rad/s, one encoder tick arrives roughly every 0.30 s, so most control cycles legitimately have no new packet. The decayed estimate will therefore fall under the stall threshold even while the wheel is moving, which will both inflate the PID error and periodically add stall boost during slow driving and docking.

2. Medium: The new hardware deadband makes the docking controller's lower-speed tuning ineffective.

   The docking config reduces `v_linear_min` from 0.20 to 0.10 m/s ([ros2_ws/src/nav2/config/explore.yaml](ros2_ws/src/nav2/config/explore.yaml#L422)), presumably to allow a gentler final approach. But the hardware write path now forces any nonzero wheel command between 0.01 and 0.35 rad/s up to 0.35 rad/s ([ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp#L316), [ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp](ros2_ws/src/diff_drive_hardware/src/diff_drive_hardware.cpp#L360)). That means the controller can no longer realize the lower end of its commanded speed range near the dock; small corrective motions will be quantized upward at the hardware boundary. In practice this undermines the config change and increases the risk of overshoot or oscillation during final alignment.

## Notes

I did not run hardware-in-the-loop tests. This review is based on the current diff plus the existing wheel firmware and ros2_control configuration.
